Musical Alphabet/Chromatic Scale

12 total notes (they span different octaves so there can be a higher pitched A and a lower pitched A)

  • 7 natural notes: A, B, C, D, E, F, G
  • 5 other notes: A♯/B♭, C♯/D♭, D♯/E♭, F♯/G♭, G♯/A♭
    • ♯ "Sharp" (higher) (ex. A♯ is higher than A)
    • ♭ "Flat" (lower) (ex. A♭ is lower than A)

Distance between notes ("Intervals"/"Steps")

  • ½ step - smallest distance between two notes (ex. A to A♯ / E to F)
    • 1 fret = ½ step
  • 1 step (ex. C to D / E to F♯)
    • 2 frets = whole step

Major Scale

7 notes

Key refers to the scale. (ex. G Major scale is the key of G)

Must have one of each note. That is when you would choose between calling a note F♯ vs G♭

Major Scale Formula: W W H W W W H
